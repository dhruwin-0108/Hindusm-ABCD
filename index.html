Arjuna

Arjuna is one of the chief protagonists of the Hindu epic Mahabharata.

Balaram

Balarama was the son of Vasudeva. and Big brother of kanha

Chaitanya

Chaitanya Mahaprabhu was a 15th-century Indian saint who is considered to be the combined avatar of Radha and Krishna.

Dwarkadhish

The Dwarka temple, also known as the Jagat Mandir. Dwarakadheesh is a Hindu temple dedicated to Krishna, or 'King of Dwarka'.

Ekalavya

Ekalavya is a character from the epic the Mahabharata. He was a young prince of the Nishadha a confederation of jungle tribes (Adivasi) in Ancient India.

Falgun

Falgun is one of the month as per Hindu calendar. The popular Hindu festival of colours Holi is celebrated in this month.

Ganga

Ganga is the personification of the river Ganges, who is worshipped by Hindus as the goddess of purification and forgiveness.

Hanuman

Hanuman is a Hindu god and a divine vanara companion of the god Rama. Hanuman is regarded to be the son of the wind-god Vayu.

Indra

Indra is the king of the gods and heaven. He is associated with the sky, lightning, weather, thunder, storms, rains, river flows, and war

Jambavan

Jambavan is described as a king of sloth bear. He was created by Brahma, to assist Rama in his struggle against Ravana.

Krishna

Krishna is the Supreme god in his own right. He is the god of protection, compassion, tenderness, and love.

Laxmana

Laxmana was the younger brother of lord Rama Laxmana also who has the signs of fortune.

Mahadev

Mahadev is one of the principal deities of Hinduism. And also known as God of Destruction.

Narasimha

Narasimha is the fourth avatar of the Hindu god Vishnu. He is regarded to have incarnated in the form of a part-lion, part-man being to slay Hiranyakashipu, and restoring dharma.

Om

Om is the prime symbol of Hinduism. It is variously said to be the essence of the supreme Absolute, consciousness atman, Brahman, or the cosmic world

Parashuram

Parashuram is the sixth avatar of god Vishnu in Hinduism. He is one of the Chiranjeevis (Immortals), who will appear at the end of the Kali Yuga to be the guru of Vishnu's tenth and last incarnation, Kalki.

Qunti

Qunti is one of the prominent characters of the Hindu epic Mahabharata. She is known as the mother of the Pandavas and Karna.

Ram

Ram is a major deity in Hinduism. He is the seventh and one of the most popular avatars of Vishnu. In Rama-centric traditions of Hinduism, he is considered the Supreme Being.

Sita

Sita is a Hindu goddess of the epic Ramayana. She is the consort of Rama. She is also the chief goddess of Rama-centric Hindu traditions. Sita is known for her dedication, self-sacrifice, courage, and purity.

Tulsi

Tulsi is a sacred plant in Hindu tradition. Hindus regard it as an earthly manifestation of the goddess Tulsi. she is regarded as the avatar of Lakshmi.

Uddhava

Uddhava is the friend and counsellor of Krishna. He plays a significant role in the Bhagavata Purana.

Vamana

Vamana is the fifth avatar of Vishnu. In the Rigveda. vamana took three strides, with which he measured out the three worlds: earth, heaven, and the space between them.

Wamika

Wamika is goddess durga. an epithet of the goddess durga, situated on the left side of vam means shiva.

xaru phool

xaru phool is use as to make some medicine in ayurved.

Yashoda

Yashoda is the mother of Krishna and the wife of Nanda.
